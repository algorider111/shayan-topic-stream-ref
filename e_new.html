📘 From Newbie to Expert in 3 Months

Based on Shayan's Method (IGM & ICPC Finalist)

👋 Introduction

  • Shayan is an International Grandmaster and ICPC World Finalist.
  • He went from Newbie to Expert in just 3 months (March–June 2015).
  • This guide captures the exact steps that helped him and his students.

✅ 1. Solve Problems Around Your Rating

  • Don't waste time on problems that are too easy or too hard.
  • Aim for ~10 problems/day at your own level (~30 mins per problem).
  • Sort problems by number of accepts and do the first 5 pages (~400 problems).
  • Focus on basic implementation, math, brute force, etc.

🚫 2. Avoid Irrelevant Advanced Topics

  • Skip complex structures like Segment Trees, Centroid Decomposition, etc.
  • These are distractions at this level.
  • Stick to solving — not memorizing algorithms.

🔁 3. Master Dynamic Programming (DP)

  • DP is essential to reach Expert level.
  • Practice variations of DP problems.
  • Understand the patterns and transitions.

🎥 4. Watch Free Topic Streams

  • Shayan provides free videos on DP, Graphs, Number Theory, etc.
  • First watch topic theory, then watch him solve problems.
  • Great for those without strong coaching.

🧠 5. Think Before Editorial

  • Keep thinking until you're completely stuck.
  • Use the editorial step-by-step — not all at once.
  • Learn from small hints instead of spoiling solutions.

📚 6. Learn While Solving

  • Don't learn algorithms first.
  • Face a problem → Learn while solving it.
  • Read other people’s codes for insight.
  • This method is how Shayan learned many advanced topics naturally.

🧪 7. Never Miss Contests

  • Participate in all live contests regularly.
  • Don’t delay for “preparation.”
  • Upsolve every unsolved contest problem after it ends.
  • Use virtual contests as mock tests anytime.

📈 Shayan's Success Formula

  • Solve 10 problems/day.
  • Learn by doing.
  • Participate in contests (live + virtual).
  • Focus on core topics: DP, Graphs, Number Theory, Greedy.
  • Don’t fear rating loss — treat it as feedback.
